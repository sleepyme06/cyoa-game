CHAPTER 8

You and Rinki stand beneath the mango tree again.
The morning rain has stopped. The earth is soft, freshly turned.
Bhoot Raja floats above the grass, shimmering faintly in the daylight. He’s humming now — not poetry, just an old song. Peaceful. Unfinished.
The teakwood box sits at your feet. Inside it: the gold. The scroll. The will. The bangle.
And your phone buzzes.
Society Head:
"We are returning with the sadhu. And reinforcements. We WILL cleanse that vault."
You have no time.
YOU CHOOSE:

  • Mukti aur Makhanwala
  • BhootTube Empire
  • Ghosts & Gulabjamuns

You turn to Bhoot Raja.
“You don’t need to stay here anymore. This isn’t just your story. It belongs to all of them. And it should be shared.”
Rinki records you reading the will aloud beneath the mango tree. She posts it online with the caption:
“Not all ghosts are scary. Some just really, really loved pulao.”
It goes viral within hours.
You donate the gold and the family recipes to a local archive.
The bungalow is restored into a museum and cultural library.
Kids on school trips giggle at the haunted bathroom.
Ghost-hunting interns take notes on the talking mango pit.
The guest bathroom is officially renamed The Emotional Cry Zone.
Bhoot Raja smiles. Faint. Glorious.
“Thank you,” he whispers. “Tell them I forgive Chhotu. And myself.”
And with that, he vanishes.
A breeze passes through the mango tree.
From then on, it blooms every year. Even in winter.
And the pulao?
It wins a National Culinary Award.
Of course it does.

NEXT

You smirk.
“You know what this house needs?”
Rinki leans in.
“What?”
“Merchandise.”
Within two weeks, you and Rinki launch BhootTube — India’s first ghost-themed content empire.
The bungalow becomes:
A paranormal co-working space
A haunted pop-up kitchen featuring Raja’s Royal Pulao
A filming location for supernatural mukbangs
You release Bhoot Raja as a virtual influencer. His voice is used in dance remixes. His sherwani becomes a fashion trend. GuruMoo is hired as your brand mascot.
The EMF Walkman sells out.
The Society tries to sue you. You buy their clubhouse and turn it into a VR ghost maze.
Bhoot Raja? He sticks around.
“You think I’d leave before my biopic drops?”
He’s even in the promotional posters.
He adds you as his emergency contact.
Rinki makes crores.
You become famous.
The house… is never quiet again.
And you’re okay with that.

NEXT

You look at the scroll. Then the mango tree. Then Bhoot Raja.
“Here’s the plan,” you say. “We bribe the Society.”
Rinki gasps.
“With ghost secrets?”
“No. With gulab jamuns and free WiFi.”
She blinks. Then nods.
Three months later…
Bhoot Bungalow reopens as a ghost-themed co-working café. Each room is named after a haunting:
The Whispering Washroom (private office)
Mango Tree Memories (podcast studio)
Ghungroo Confession Zone (Zoom booths)
Guests get:
Discounted ghost naps
Mango-scented tea
A loyalty program called “Bhoot Points”
The Society?
Totally bribed. They get lifetime tea refills and custom slippers.
GuruMoo?
Now runs the Instagram page.
Bhoot Raja stays. He gives TED Talks.
Mostly about poetry. Sometimes about taxes.
You?
You finally sleep in the bungalow and don’t get haunted.
(Okay. Maybe once. But it was just the laddu trying to spoon you.)
