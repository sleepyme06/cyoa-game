CHAPTER 4

You’re in the courtyard, trying to unlock a rusted lockbox using brute force and a mild tantrum.
Rinki arrives, riding pillion on a scooty driven by an auto-wala she claims is “ghost-resistant.”
She hops off with a camera, a modified Walkman, and enough chewing gum to build a small fort.
“Okay, genius,” she says. “You’ve got a haunted house and some kind of Bollywood ghost. Let’s go full paranormal investigation mode.”
You roll your eyes but let her in.
Within minutes:
She reprograms her EMF Walkman into a spectral scanner.
It leads you to a cracked stone tile near the prayer room.
You lift it to reveal a sealed passage and a note etched into the stone.
"Teakwood Vault – Access only through bloodline, betrayal, and bhindi."
You both stare at each other.
“Did he say bhindi?”
“We’ll pretend it means something.”
Then Rinki finds something big: behind the tulsi pot, the EMF screams. She unearths:
A burnt legal deed, referencing “Raj Mahal Wing – Teakwood Vault – NOT FOR CHHOTU”
A faded photo of two men standing under a mango tree—one looks like Bhoot Raja, the other is labeled: “Chhotu (he blinked)”
A page from a journal signed... Chhotu Babu, dated 1947
It reads:
“He trusted me. And I locked it away. I couldn’t tell him. The war was coming. I thought I’d have time.”
Suddenly, the tulsi pot topples on its own, revealing a small brass key.
The key glows briefly, then cools.
“Okay,” Rinki mutters. “Either this is a Disney Channel Original Movie... or we’re in way deeper than we thought.”
You:

  • Use the key on the nearby wall panel and keep it rational
  • Take the journal page and seek ghost context
  • Give Rinki the laddu and see what explodes

CHAPTER 4

The scroll leads you to the old library, where the walls smell of decaying pages and secret resentment.
Bhoot Raja appears with flair, floating sideways for no reason.
“Ah! The library. Where words once wept.”
You push past a shelf. Behind it: a hidden room lined with strange objects.
Each one glows faintly.
You find:
A brass bell with the engraving:
“Strike only where silence rules.”
A mango pickle jar that hums when touched
A cracked floor tile under which lies a letter, wrapped in a silk handkerchief
The letter is from Raja’s wife.
“You always loved riddles more than me. I’ve hidden your inheritance beneath the mango roots. But first, unlock your shame.”
The pickle jar jiggles violently. You unscrew the lid.
It screams again:
“HE LIED! CHHOTU KNEW!”
Raja winces.
“Ignore the mango. She’s dramatic.”
But you can’t ignore the blueprint drawn in turmeric on the back of the letter.
It shows:
A buried vault under the east wing
Symbols matching the bell, the ghungroo, and the number 1947
A warning: “Do not speak the name Chhotu Babu within its walls”
You whisper the name anyway.
The bell rings by itself. The walls shake. A book bursts into flames.
“YOU HAD ONE JOB,” Bhoot Raja screams as he panics into smoke form.
You:

  • Rebuild the burnt blueprint using science
  • Run downstairs to find someone who’s good with puzzles
  • Yell “GULABJAMUN123” into the bell to see what happens

CHAPTER 4

You return to the east wing, guided by the laddu's now erratic buzzing.
Badshah Bhoot waits.
He’s wearing a silk cape, sunglasses, and somehow has a fan blowing wind at him.
“Welcome back, mortal. Ready to host the drama of the century?”
“What... are we doing exactly?”
“Riddle Duel. Winner gets access to the memory well.”
Before you can object, a circle of floating pickles surrounds you. It’s a stage.
A harmonium plays itself. Bhoot Raja floats in dramatically.
“Let’s get this over with.”
The duel begins. You are the judge. You have no rules to follow.
The riddles escalate:
Badshah:
“What walks on four legs at dawn, two at noon, three after Rasgulla?”
(You respond: “A man with diabetes.”)
Raja:
“What lives in silence but whispers in steam?”
(You respond: “Upma.” He sobs.)
Suddenly, the trophy — the one they’ve fought over for 80 years — levitates from the wall and implodes into gold dust.
The pickles collapse.
A voice echoes:
“Enough theatrics. The vault awaits.”
The laddu glows red. It spins. Then flings itself through the air and lands in a hidden compartment behind the stage.
Inside, you find:
A box labeled: “Gulabjamun123”
A key wrapped in silk
A ghostly wedding invitation:
Raja Ram Prasad & Mystery Guest
You raise an eyebrow.
Raja winces.
“We don’t talk about that evening.”
You:

  • Open the box and see what flies out
  • Take the invitation to decode its implications
  • Bring the key to Rinki to make a plan
