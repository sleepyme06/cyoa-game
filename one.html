CHAPTER 1

Summer was supposed to be epic.
Science camp. Zip lines. Frog dissections. Campfire chemistry tricks. And most importantly—a glorious, screen-free experience that your parents promised would help you “reconnect with reality.”
Instead, reality punched you in the face.
Somewhere between registration errors, a flash flood, or divine sabotage, camp was cancelled. No refunds. No apologies. Just a sudden suitcase shoved into your arms and your mom cheerfully announcing:
“You’ll be staying at Mamaji’s house this summer! In the village. He’s abroad. Fresh air will be good for you.”
Good for you, apparently, meant exile.
Now you’re standing in front of said house—a sprawling, crumbling creature of stone and regret, soaked in monsoon gloom. A narrow path of cracked tiles leads through a garden that looks like it’s plotting something.
Your sneakers are soaked. Mosquitoes orbit your head like vultures. You're holding your suitcase in one hand and a badminton racket in the other because, well, ghosts probably respect sports equipment.
The rusted iron gate creaks open. You didn’t touch it.
Of course you didn’t.
You step forward.
A crow caws at you. Directly at you. Judgmentally.
“Yeah, I hate it too,” you mutter.
The house looms.
It’s tall, sagging, and entirely too enthusiastic about being spooky. Moss clings to its corners like guilt. Shutters rattle with dramatic flair. You half-expect thunder to strike just to complete the aesthetic.
A chill skates down your spine as you approach the front door. Not fear. Not yet. Just... that feeling you get when you realize your summer vacation is going to suck.
NEXT

The front porch is made of old teakwood and fresh despair. Your suitcase thuds against the steps as you climb them. A broken nameplate swings above you:
“SHANTI NIVAS”
Yeah, right.
You raise your hand to knock, but the door creaks open before you can touch it.
There’s no one there.
There’s nothing there, except stale air that smells like turmeric, rain, and unresolved trauma.
You step inside.
The door slams shut behind you.
“Classic,” you mutter. “Haunted house behavior within five minutes. Great start.”
Inside, it’s... worse.
The air is heavy, like the walls are holding their breath. Faded family portraits glare from the walls. A cracked chandelier sways slightly, though there’s no breeze. Somewhere in the distance, a harmonium plays a note that sounds like a sigh.
You pass a large mirror. Your reflection blinks half a second too slow.
Okay, that’s new.
You shake it off and move toward the staircase. Everything groans under your weight—floorboards, railings, your will to live.
At the landing above, three hallways branch out in opposite directions.
And then you hear it.
From the left: a whispering voice behind a locked door.
From the right: a rickety trapdoor to the attic swings open, inviting.
Straight ahead: something crashes. Then giggles. Multiple. None of them belong to you.
NEXT

You take a deep breath.
You're not a believer. Not exactly. But this house is definitely... active.
Your phone, previously fully charged, now displays 13% battery. The WiFi network is named BHOOT_4G but requires a password you’ll never guess.
You notice three objects at your feet. You definitely didn’t see them before:
A dusty old notebook, its pages scribbled in multiple languages, diagrams, and codes.
A copper bell, warm to the touch despite the rain.
A half-empty Frooti, chilled, with a straw already in it.
You slowly look around.
There’s no one else here.
Your fingers twitch. Your brain itches. Your summer just got very weird, very fast.

Choose Your Path — and Your Doom

Your journey begins now.
But remember—paths may cross. What you ignore now may haunt you later.
Choose wisely.

  • Explore cautiously. Focus on rational things. Ignore the attic—for now.
  • Open that attic. Ask questions. Get answers.
  • Yell “BHOOT BHAIYAA, KYA HAL HAI?” and touch everything.
