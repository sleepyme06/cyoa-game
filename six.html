CHAPTER 6

Setting: Bhoot Bungalow, mid-monsoon.

The vault is nearly uncovered. You hold secrets, clues, and maybe a ghost contract. And then—your phone pings.
It begins with a text from Rinki:
RINKI:
“Uhhh. We’ve got visitors. Like, A LOT. Also... a cow just tried to headbutt me. Send help?”
You peek through the cracked window.
The Society President has arrived.
And he’s brought reinforcements:
A Bluetooth-enabled sadhu, broadcasting mantras at max volume from his phone.
Three aunties with steel tiffins, full of judgment and very spicy murmura.
And, most ominously, a divine cow named GuruMoo, draped in marigold chains and righteous fury.
Rinki rushes inside, slamming the door.
“They think we’ve disturbed a ghost-temple-zone. Apparently Mamaji’s house is now under ‘cultural siege’.”
Bhoot Raja appears mid-air, twirling dramatically.
“They cannot silence me. I am poetry incarnate!”
You try to explain the situation to the Society President—who refuses to cross the threshold.
“We are invoking Residential Code Section 13-Bhoo. No ghost play, no underground digging, and absolutely no drama-based haunting after 5pm.”
The Bluetooth sadhu nods solemnly and begins chanting “Ghost Mukt Bharat” from a SoundCloud playlist.
You:

  • Perform a fake exorcism to trick the Society into leaving
  • Sneak into the Society President’s car and steal his blueprints
  • Use Bhoot Raja’s scroll to decode the ancient ghost contract

You and Rinki rig the EMF reader to a smoke machine.
Bhoot Raja performs a dramatic exit monologue.
GuruMoo gets offered a banana. Everyone leaves peacefully.
NEXT

Rinki distracts the sadhu by asking about astrology.
You crawl under tiffin shadows and snatch a folder labeled “Structural Maps – Mamaji’s Vault”.
Inside: a second entrance to the vault. Beneath the guest bathroom.

GuruMoo moos approvingly.
