CHAPTER 3

You’ve decided to keep things normal. Or at least, normal-adjacent. Ignoring the glowing ghosts, magic laddu, and poetry battles seems like the healthiest choice.
You return to the servant’s quarters.
There, you find a hidden compartment behind a crooked portrait. Inside:
A moldy diary, written in Tamil and Hindi, soaked with old oil stains.
A sketch of a mango tree with an underground passage marked.
And stuck to the last page: a scrap of newspaper from 1946. Headline reads:
"LOCAL ACCOUNTANT ACCUSED OF THEFT — CHHOTU BABU MISSING."
The floor creaks behind you.
You spin.
It’s Rinki, filming with her phone and chewing gum like she’s trying to intimidate the wallpaper.
“Is that... a ghost clue? You found one without me?!”
“I’m not even sure if—”
“Shush. We’re going viral.”
She rewires her EMF Walkman, which immediately starts making static shrieks in front of the old tulsi pot in the courtyard.
You dig beneath it and discover:
A lockbox (padlocked, rusted)
A burnt legal document partially signed “...Teakwood Vault, Raja Ram Pras...”
A crumpled napkin with “Chhotu Babu <3 Kulfi” written on it
Rinki squints.
“Wait. Teakwood Vault. That’s real. It’s in Mamaji’s plans. I saw it once.”
“You read building plans?”
“I read everything. I’m emotionally attached to blueprints.”
Then, your phone buzzes with a voice note. It's static... and a poem:
“Jahan dopehar ki roshni bhi thak jaaye,
vahan baith ke sach roya tha.”
Rinki goes pale.
“That’s the bathroom.”
You:

  • Refuse to believe a haunted bathroom matters
  • Recognize the poetic voice as Bhoot Raja’s
  • Let Rinki record you dramatically yelling in the toilet

CHAPTER 3

You’re elbows-deep in ancient scrolls, translating metaphors about mangoes and betrayal.
Bhoot Raja reappears in bursts of ghazal and ghee. He floats by a wall panel and mutters:
“Chhotu always hid things beneath sweet things... and under brass.”
You investigate.
Behind a shelf of brass utensils, you find:
A brass bell with an engraving underneath:
“Strike me at dawn, for truth to ring.”
A pickled mango jar labeled: “Property of Raja Ram Prasad – Do Not Consume (Seriously).”
It vibrates when you ask, “Where’s the vault?”
You cautiously unscrew the lid.
The mango hums.
Then it screams:
“Ghooooooossttt vaaauuulllttt! Chaubey locked it!”
You fall over. The mango continues humming, smugly.
Elsewhere, under a cracked floorboard, you find a letter in invisible ink:
“Chhotu, I did not betray you. I only meant to protect you. Saheb never trusted me.”
Raja floats beside you, tearfully.
“They were in love. That complicates things.”
“What?!”
“Yes. Forbidden. Tragic. Mango-filled. Our servant drama was far ahead of its time.”
You sit down, overwhelmed.
The final clue lies tied in the ghungroo of a shattered Kathak statue in the music room. The ankle bells hum faintly. When you touch them, you see a vision of:
The vault
The mango tree
A man crying while holding a laddu
You blink. The room returns to normal.
You:

  • Keep solving the poetry-based riddles
  • Start fact-checking with Rinki
  • Ask the screaming mango more questions

CHAPTER 3

You awaken from a nap on the memory trunk, surrounded by sparkles and the sound of distant tabla solos.
Bhoot Raja floats beside you, wearing sunglasses he did not have earlier.
“You’ve done it. We’re now memory-linked. Also I borrowed your socks while you slept.”
He tosses you your socks. They’re mildly ectoplasmic.
You now have access to:
The ghost cloak, upgraded (now slightly more invisible)
The vibrating laddu, glowing faintly red
The memory projector, which only activates during naps or severe boredom
Together, you sneak through the house like dramatic fools.
Then you accidentally awaken…
Badshah Bhoot.
A rival ghost. Stuck in the east wing. Wearing 40 pounds of drama.
“RAAAJAAAA! YOU STILL OWE ME A TROPHY!”
“It was rigged, you jalebi-brained thespian!”
“I DEMAND A RE-MATCH!”
Suddenly, your entire world becomes:
A pun-based riddle duel between ghosts
Floating pickle jars attacking from all angles
You dodging a floating harmonium with a scream-laugh soundtrack
Eventually, Badshah’s cursed poetry trophy is found buried in the planter. It glows. You touch it.
A memory flares:
The vault door
The number: Gulabjamun123
A mango tree. Again. Always the mango tree.
Bhoot Raja whispers:
“We’re getting close.”
You high-five the ghost.
It passes through your hand and knocks over a vase.
You:

  • Nap again for another vision
  • Ask what "Gulabjamun123" really unlocks
  • Go find Rinki and tell her everything
